Trees

Trees are tall, woody plants that usually live for many years. Examples include Oak, Pine, and Baobab.

  • Oak (Quercus)
  • Pine (Pinus)
  • Baobab (Adansonia)
  • Maple (Acer)
  • Sequoia (Sequoiadendron)

Flowers

Flowers are the reproductive parts of flowering plants. Examples include Rose, Tulip, and Orchid.

  • Rose (Rosa)
  • Tulip (Tulipa)
  • Orchid (Orchidaceae)
  • Sunflower (Helianthus)
  • Lily (Lilium)

Shrubs

Shrubs are shorter, woody plants often found in gardens and landscapes. Examples include Azalea, Lavender, and Holly.

  • Azalea (Rhododendron)
  • Lavender (Lavandula)
  • Holly (Ilex)
  • Hydrangea (Hydrangeaceae)
  • Boxwood (Buxus)

Ferns

Ferns are non-flowering plants that reproduce via spores. Examples include Bracken, Maidenhair, and Staghorn Fern.

  • Bracken (Pteridium)
  • Maidenhair Fern (Adiantum)
  • Staghorn Fern (Platycerium)
  • Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata)
  • Tree Fern (Cyatheales)

Succulents

Succulents are plants with thick, fleshy tissues adapted to water storage. Examples include Aloe, Cactus, and Agave.

  • Aloe (Aloe vera)
  • Barrel Cactus (Ferocactus)
  • Agave (Agave americana)
  • Echeveria (Echeveria spp.)
  • Jade Plant (Crassula ovata)

Grasses

Grasses are monocotyledonous plants often found in meadows, savannas, and lawns. Examples include Bamboo, Wheat, and Ryegrass.

  • Bamboo (Bambusoideae)
  • Wheat (Triticum)
  • Ryegrass (Lolium)
  • Bluegrass (Poa)
  • Switchgrass (Panicum virgatum)
